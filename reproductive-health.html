Planned Parenthood

Planned Parenthood is a leading provider of sexual and reproductive health care in the United States, operating nearly 600 health centers nationwide. They offer a broad range of services including birth control, abortion care, STI testing and treatment, cancer screenings, pregnancy-related care, gender-affirming services, and preventive health exams. Additionally, they provide comprehensive sex education programs to millions of people annually, promoting informed and healthy choices.

Power to Decide

Power to Decide is a national, non-partisan nonprofit organization dedicated to advancing sexual and reproductive well-being for all, with a particular focus on contraception and abortion access. Originally founded as The National Campaign to Prevent Teen Pregnancy, the organization now works to ensure that everyone regardless of who they are or where they live-has the power to decide if, when, and under what circumstances to get pregnant and have a child. Power to Decide provides evidence-based sexual health information, supports comprehensive reproductive health services, and develops digital resources like Bedsider and AbortionFinder to help people access care and information.

IPAs

Independent Physician Associations (IPAs), also known as Independent Practice Associations, are organizations formed by networks of independent physicians or healthcare providers who come together to contract collectively with health plans, managed care organizations, or employers. While maintaining their private practices, these physicians use the IPA structure to negotiate service agreements, participate in value-based care programs, and access shared resources such as information technology, administrative support, and care coordination tools. IPAs can include primary care doctors, specialists, and even providers of behavioral health or social services, depending on their focus and membership.
